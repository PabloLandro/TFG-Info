Imitrex (Sumatriptan)

Imitrex is the brand name for sumatriptan, a prescription drug used to treat migraines in adults.
Imitrex is best taken when the headache pain starts – not before, even though there are warning signals like aura (sensory or visual disturbances). It is for migraine with or without.
Imitrex is a serotonin receptor agonist. It operates by brainstem and decreasing elements that may trigger nausea, light sensitivity, as well as other migraine symptoms.

Common Negative Effects of Imitrex
The most frequently reported side effects of Imitrex are:
Fatigue, weakness, drowsiness
Dizziness
Problems
If these don’t go away or bother you, contact your physician.
Serious Negative Effects of Imitrex
A few of the negative effects of Imitrex are severe. Phone your physician immediately in the event you notice the following:
Changes in the sense or color of fingers or toes (Raynaud’s syndrome)
Bloody diarrhea, constipation, nausea, and vomiting, extreme stomach pain
Cramping, pain, tingling in legs, hips, leg muscles, feet, toes
Urticaria, swelling of tongue, throat region
Mental changes, hallucinations
Pain
Stiffness in neck
Uncommon Unwanted Effects of Imitrex
