Anal Skin Tag
Definition
Anal Skin Tags also known as acrochordon and cutaneous papillomae are shapeless bumps of flesh that are commonly located at the anal verge. It is basically at the area where the inner side of anorectal canal becomes the outer side of the body.
Anal skin tags may occur in multiple numbers or alone and is non-contagious.
Causes of Anal Skin Tag
Anorectic injury, inflammatory eruptions and trauma can lead to starting of anal skin tags.
Example: curing the large swelled up external hemorrhoid.
Signs and Symptoms of Anal Skin Tag
No such symptom is seen in anal skin tags. However people become aware of their existence during wiping or cleaning.
