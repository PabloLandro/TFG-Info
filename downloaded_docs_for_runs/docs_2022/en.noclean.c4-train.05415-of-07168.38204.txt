Are We Drinking Too much Water?
It is supposed to be one of lifes natural goodness, but now experts are warning that drinking too much water could be bad for us.
By Hannah Gilchrist1
13/07/2011
Scientists claim that the goodness in drinking lots of water is actually a myth, and the recommended eight glasses of water a day could be doing more harm than good.
The turnaround comes as researchers dismiss the power of dehydration, and that drinking more water can prevent multiple health problems. In fact, other studies have claimed that too much water can cause kidney damage, instead of preventing it.
Dr McCartney, who worked on the study, wrote in the British Medical Journal that drinking when you are not thirsty can impair concentration, and that consuming too much water can lead to a hyponatraemia, a rare but fatal condition which sees salt levels in the body fall.
