Learn How to Exercise for Back Pain Learn How to Exercise for Back Pain It may seem odd to exercise for back pain, but it is one of the best ways to strengthen the muscles of your back so that you avoid straining your back which is often the cause of back pain. Weak muscles are often at the root of pain in the back and this is especially true of the lower back area. Your buttocks, abdomen and back muscles are there to support your spine. These are referred to as core muscles. The spine uses these muscles as a defense mechanism against the pull of gravity. When you strengthen those muscles, it can eliminate, prevent and reduce back pain. Strong abdominal muscles are as vital as having back muscles that are strong in order to support your lower back. The muscles in the front of your thighs called quadriceps need to be strong as well so that you decrease the chance of injuring your back when you are doing heavy lifting. We all know that for proper lifting techniques one should use their legs, but if the muscles of the legs are not strong, most of us end up using our backs to lift a heavy load and this may cause a sprain. Muscles that are Short Can also Cause Back Pain When muscles are shortened, it can throw your spine out of position and this will definitely cause you to experience back pain. Exercises for back pain that stress stretching techniques can lengthen muscles that are shortened and relieve any pain you may be experiencing in your lower back. The muscles at the back of the thighs called hamstrings, quadriceps, and the muscles of the buttocks and back can all affect the alignment of your spine. The joints of the spine will benefit with increased mobility when using stretching exercises for back pain as well. The exercises you do to strengthen your back need not be of a vigorous kind. Simple pelvic tilts, the basic twist, bringing knees to chest and exercises for back pain along those lines can do a world of good when it comes to strengthening your core muscles. Yoga is often a great way to strengthen muscles while helping your inner spirit to become balanced. Some yoga positions may seem impossible at first, but as you practice and stick with this type of gentle exercise for back pain, before long your back problems will ease and your flexibility and peace of mind will increase. Always remember, before starting any type of exercise program you should always consult with your health care provider to ensure that the program you choose will not be detrimental to your health and well being. As we mentioned, there is no need to join an aerobics class which may cause you even more back pain. Simple and gentle exercises for back pain that will strengthen the core muscles will have you feeling not only free of back pain, but your overall health and fitness will benefit greatly as well.
